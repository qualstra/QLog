Greatship Rohini was operating as a Platform Supply Vessel with ONGC performing Platform Supply & support duties for its rigs and platforms on the West Coast of Mumbai.

This inspection & condition assessment is based on IMCA CMID (IMCA M I 49 Issue 10) with additional check points undertaken with reference to the Greatship (India) Ltd. internal operating standards and policy documents. Objective of the exercise is also to ensure that the maintenance & management system onboard the vessel is effective for her to keep performing well with zero incidents and meet the Organizations ’s business objectives. It is also to ensure that the maintenance system followed on board is adequate and satisfactory to provide good condition of the vessel on a sustainable basis. The final objective is to remain the fore runner in the Offshore Industry and meet entire expectations of our clients.

2Executive Summary

This is the forth CAS Inspection for Greatship Rohini. Vessel begun operations with ONGC as PSV from end of Apr 2018. The Last CAS inspection was carried out in the month of March 2019. The vessel’s general condition was found to good and good maintenance standard was being followed. Given below is a comparison of the CAS scores.

Rohini CAS Scores

Vessels staff seemed well aware of the company’s SMS and noticeable efforts could be seen in the maintenance carried out onboard. Machinery spaces were found in satisfactory condition. It was quite impressive to see the space around the Main Engine, CPP, Gearbox in dry and clean condition indicating no leaks from the running machinery. We hope that the vessel staff will continue with the regular maintenance to sustain the condition witnessed.

However, some observations were made during the inspection which are recommended to be attended to for closure. All the observations listed are not necessarily considered as deficiencies but scope for improvement.

The Vessel Master & Chief Engineer must ensure that the vessel staff should remain motivated and continue doing the good work. Standards in the vessel upkeep should not be compromised and all shore based support to be continued to be provided in order to fulfil this goal.
