6. CONDITION REPORT

Score is:



Technical Condition Grading

Technical Condition Grading is based on the scope of inspection possible within the limitations prevailing at the time such as access, time available, and ship staff support during the inspection.
Rating must be evaluated in conjunction with the report for corrective action.

Very Poor: Vessel Maintenance and management is below standards; Lacks minimal stores and spares; Vessel not in safe operating condition, extensive work scope in critical areas has to be planned to re-instate the sea-worthiness of the vessel.

Poor: Vessel condition and maintenance is below acceptable standards, Extensive planned repair required immediately.

Below Average: Condition of vessel is marginally below acceptable standards and major areas require immediate corrections and repair as remedy.

Average: Vessel is in average standards, rectifications and repair on immediate basis in pointed out areas is essential to raise the rating.

Above Average: Several "usual/routine deficiencies, some of which require immediate corrective action. All highlighted areas need attention.

Fair: Vessel requires routine repair and maintenance in a planned fashion to either remain at this state or to improve existing standards.

Good: Vessel is well maintained and managed with minor but non-critical deficiencies. Normal planned maintenance and stringent follow-up will ensure vessel standards to be maintained.

Excellent: Vessel is in un-impaired condition, with excellent maintenance and management.
